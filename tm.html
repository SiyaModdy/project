    Table Mountain!

    Brief History

    Hoerikwaggo, which translates to "Peak in the Sea," is the name given to the mountain by the local Khoisan people of the Cape. The first white person to climb the Mountain was the Portuguese adventurer Antionio de Saldanha. In 1503, he did so and gave the mountain the name Taboa de Cabo. This signifies the "Table of the Cape."

    Following this, Dutch immigrants began referring to it as Tafelberg, which translates to Table Mountain, starting around 1652. Nowadays, the mountain is a destination in South Africa and it part of the 7 wonders of the world.

    Cable Car

    To summit table mountain one can either hike or use the cable car. The first cable car was built in 1929 and could only hold 25 people; today, it can hold up to 60 passengers. The city and the Atlantic Ocean are visible from the top.

    Nature

    The majority of the plant and animal species found on Table Mountain are indigenous, and they are very diverse. This implies that they cannot be found elsewhere. The national park contains 8,200 different plant types. The rock hyrax, often known as the dassie, is the most prevalent animal on the mountain.
